    The institution's principal is said to have sought the services of the deceased after Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) disconnected power to the school over a huge unpaid electricity bill.

    However, in the Monday, May 24, incident, the man identified as Felix Odoyo, was electrocuted in the process and his lifeless body got stuck on the pole, according to images seen by TUKO.co.ke.

    Confirming the incident, the area chief said the principal called him at around 1.30pm informing him of the incident.

    He further confirmed that it was the principal who called and authorised Odoyo to illegally reconnect the power.

    According to the family's 'spokesman' Charles Odhiambo, their kin did not have papers or anything to show that he had technical know-how in handling electricity, faulting the principal for his untimely death.

    "Once the KPLC and police are done with their part, we as the family will also demand answers from the school administration because the boy does not have any papers. How can a layman come in the school on abroad daylight and try to reconnect power that KPLC had disconnected? posed Odhiambo.
