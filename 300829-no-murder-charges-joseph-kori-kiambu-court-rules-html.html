    No murder charges for Joseph Kori - Kiambu court rules

    - Mary Wambui is reported to have been fatally injured when she got into a fight with Kori's lover Wangui - Police arrested the two lovers on Saturday, January 26, in connection to the mysterious murder

    11 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - Mary Wambui is reported to have been fatally injured when she got into a fight with Kori's lover Wangui

    - Police arrested the two lovers on Saturday, January 26, in connection to the mysterious murder

    - The mother of two was buried in Nyeri county on Saturday, March 3

    - Kori described his wife as an irreplaceable woman who was in her prime

    A man who was arrested alongside his lover over the mysterious murder of his wife has been set free.

    Joseph Kori who was nabbed in connection with Mary Wambui's murder will now testify against his lover .

    Kori who appeared before Kiambu Senior Principal Magistrate Stella Atambo on Friday, March 15, was released unconditionally after the prosecution team failed to place him at the scene of crime.

    As a result, the magistrate closed his file upon taking into account all submissions and applications.

    "The first respondent is hereby discharged unconditionally since he has been indicated as prosecution witness and this file is marked as concluded. You are free to go," ruled Atambo.

    The businessman was among the first key suspects to be arrested following the brutal murder of his wife and subsequently spent close to a month in police custody.

    Kori was picked up together with his secret lover Judy Wangui for allegedly killing his wife Wambui on Saturday, January 26.

    The deceased is reported to have been fatally injured when she got into a fight with Wangui at her Fourways Junction home after the two ladies had a good time together for the better part of that Saturday.

    During her burial on Saturday, March 3, Kori occasionally sunk into a trance, maybe reflecting on the void left by the painful departure of the apple of his eye.

    “You are irreplaceable. With your departure I feel a part of me has been taken away. Your charm, jovial mood and readiness to assist everyone will always be remembered. I want to assure you of my undivided and unconditional love to our sons,” mourned the man.
