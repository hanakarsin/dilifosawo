    Chaos at City Hall as MCAs clash over fresh plot to impeach Speaker Elachi

    There was chaos at the Nairobi City county assembly on Tuesday, October 29, after two rival groups of MCAs clashed over a plot to impeach Speaker Beatrice Elachi.

    It all began during the presser by a section of Jubilee MCAs who were attacked by another faction which poured water on them while shouting, disrupting the event.

    The pro-Elachi group was the first to address the media within the assembly precincts, where it was reacting on the planned fresh impeachment.

    The city politicians who are opposed to return of Elachi had on Monday, October 28, announced plans to unseat her.

    Nairobi West MCA Maurice Gari, also a member of the Public Service Board, said that no ODM member would support the ouster motion.

    “As ODM members, we support the party position that Elachi is the legitimate Speaker, no member will go against that,” said Gari.

    Embakasi Ward MCA Michael Ogada said that whoever was fighting the Speaker was against the handshake.

    His sentiments were shared by his Hamza counterpart Mark Ndung’u who read ill motive in the latest development even after the ruling party gave a directive on the matter.

    “We met at the party headquarters and the decision was Elachi is the Speaker, I'm now wondering why some members have chosen to go against the party’s decision,” said Ndung’u.

    Elachi was ousted by MCAs in 2018 on grounds that she was abusing office, engaging in graft and had questionable academic qualifications, allegations she refuted as petty politics.

    During the censure motion, 103 out of 107 MCAs voted for her removal from office.

    Story by J.Papanoo,TUKO.co.ke correspondent in Nairobi county.
