    A Nigerian man has decried the decline in newspapers sales following the advancement of social media in recent times.

    Mallam Bawa, a newspaper vendor, told BBC News Pidgin that he has been in the business for the past 51 years.

    After the Nigerian civil war, the man who ventured into the business said that the trade had transformed his life for the better.

    The Kano resident said he owns a house, he is married and can cater for his 15 children through the proceeds from the newspaper selling business.

    Netizens react to calls of embracing newspapers

    The vendor's call urging Nigerians to start embracing newspapers and stop relying on the internet has sparked varied reaction on social media platforms.

    @xwaggaruns advised the old man to consider embracing the digital era saying that things were fast changing globally.

    "You have to adapt to the change. Even electric cars will soon start cruising in Nigeria meaning petrol will be worthless. This will mark the end of Nigerian politics on the international stage,’’ @xwaggaruns wrote.

    @sparklesjewelries said that he was touched after the old man revealed that no newspaper company had appreciated his efforts.

    "I was particularly touched by the part where he said that no newspaper company has come out after all these years to appreciate his efforts leave alone celebrating his many years in service,’’ said @sparklesjewelries.

    ‘’It is good that at least he did something commendable with the money from his earnings. It is high time that his children start taking good care of him as well,’’ @toesscene wrote.

    Slain newspaper vendor's wife rejects KSh 131 million demand

    Meanwhile, TUKO.co.ke previously reported wife of a slain newspaper vendor rejected her in-law's KSh 131 million demand from House of Representatives Speaker Femi Gbajabiamila.

    Okereke urged Gbajabiamila to ignore her in-law’s demand and fulfil his promises to her and her two children.
