    Dare I say, everyone on the planet knows who The Man of Steel is. The fictional superhero first appeared in American comic books published by DC Comics, and was created by writer Jerry Siegel and artist Joe Shuster. Today, many Superman movies, comics, cartoons, and TV shows have been created, and multitudes of actors and voice actors have played the popular comic book superhero. Ever wondered how many Superman actors have played the superhero? Find out by reading this post about the actors who played Superman.

    Check out this extensive list of Superman actors.

    Top Superman actors ranked

    Ever wondered who played The Man of Steel the best? Check out this list of the actors who have portrayed the superhero.

    1. Bud Collyer

    Bud Collyer was the first actor to portray The Man of Steel in the media (both on the radio and in the classic cartoons from the Fleischer and Famous studios).

    The actor played The Man of Steel for roughly 2000 radio episodes that aired between 1940 and 1951.

    He then returned to the role in 1966 for Filmation’s The New Adventures of Superman animated series.

    As the only Superman of radio, the silver screen, and television, he has logged more hours as the Man of Steel than any actor in history.

    He played the superhero in:

    • The New Adventures of Superman (1966-1968)
    • Aquaman (1967)
    • The Superman/Aquaman Hour of Adventure (1967)
    • The Batman/Superman Hour (1968)

    2. Tom Welling

    Tom Welling starred on Smallville from 2001-2011. Smallville told the tale of a teenage Clark Kent and his eventual destiny as Metropolis' resident superhero.

    Welling got to play The Man of Steel again during the Arrowverse's mammoth Crisis on Infinite Earths crossover event in 2019.

    3. Henry Cavill

    4. Michael Daingerfield

    The actor played The Man of Steel in 2005’s Krypto: The Superdog.

    5. Kevin Caliber

    Actor Kevin Caliber portrayed the character in the opening credits of Supergirl‘s first season. He then he portrayed the character in the 2019 independent film Supermen: World War.

    6. Jeff Kramer

    Kramer had his most prominent role in 2002's Superman: The Man Of Steel.

    7. John Haymes Newton

    Gerard Christopher played the character for an impressive 73 half-hour episodes of Superboy between 1989 and 1992.

    In 1988, the British actor started voicing The Metropolis Marvel in a variety of radio productions based on different story arcs from DC Comics.     In a Men's’Health December cover story, Cavill revealed he had not given up the role.

    “The cape is still in the closet,” he said. “It’s still mine. I’m not going to sit quietly in the dark as all the stuff is going on. I’ve not given up the role. There’s a lot I have to give for Superman yet. A lot of storytelling to do. A lot of real, true depths to the honesty of the character I want to get into. I want to reflect the comic books. That’s important to me. There’s a lot of justice to be done for Superman. The status is: You’ll see.”
