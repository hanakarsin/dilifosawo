    Moses Kuria declares he will vie for presidency in 2022, picks Raila as preferred deputy

    - Kuria expressed confidence he would win the top seat by a wide margin - He said he had the experience and skills required to lead Kenya after Uhuru retires - The MP had earlier indicated he may ditch Jubilee for Democratic Party (DP)

    31 May 2024, 00:00

    - Kuria expressed confidence he would win the top seat by a wide margin

    - He said he had the experience and skills required to lead Kenya after Uhuru retires

    - The MP had earlier indicated he may ditch Jubilee for Democratic Party (DP)

    - The Gatundu South MP would face off with several other candidates including DP Ruto

    - Ironically, Kuria picked Ruto as one of his preferred running mates, others being Raila and Kibwana

    Gatundu South Member of Parliament, Moses Kuria, has reiterated his interest in succeeding President Uhuru Kenyatta whose two terms is expected to end in 2022.

    The controversial lawmaker indicated he was ready to take on Deputy President William Ruto who is perceived to be the top contender among the politicians planning to compete in the 2022 presidential race.

    Kuria who was speaking on Hot 96 on Monday, February 4, reaffirmed his plans to vie for the presidency and also for the first time revealed names of his preferred running mates.

    The MP said he would consider Opposition leader Raila Odinga, Makueni governor Kivutha Kibwana, or DP Ruto, ironically, as his deputy.

    This is the second time Kuria announced he would be taking a stab at the top most political seat.

    The outspoke MP on October 22, 2018, made a similar declaration during which he also said he would support himself for the presidency and expressed confidence he would carry the day.

    "I had six opponents against me in Gatundu South and I won by 93%. I know my stuff and I do not believe in losing. I am very poor at that," he said then.

    READ ALSO: Majority of Kenyans cannot stand a Moses Kuria presidency - Poll

    Kuria who has in the recent past become critical of Uhuru's administration had also days earlier hinted at competing for the top office on the Democratic Party (DP) ticket.

    TUKO.co.ke understands he would, however, have to exit the ruling Jubilee Party, a move that would consequently trigger a by-election in Gatundu South constituency.

    Interestingly, the DP's national chairman, Esau Kioni, former president Mwai Kibaki's security advisor, is said to be also salivating for the top job and may have to battle for the party;s flag with other candidates including Kuria.

    READ ALSO: Uhuru Kenyatta blasts youth leadership, says older people performing better

    Besides Ruto, the other presidential hopefuls who have publicly declared interest in the 2022 race include Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua, Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi, Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka and Ford Kenya boss Moses Wetangula.

    Although a section of Kenyans believe ODM party leader, Raila, would also be on the ballot once again, the former prime minister is yet to declare his interest in succeeding Uhuru.
