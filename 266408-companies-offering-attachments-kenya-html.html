    Attachments or internships are temporary work placements designed to help college or university students learn the practical aspects of a job. Often, these programs are unpaid, but some companies provide attachés or interns small stipends for travel costs, meals, etc. This article shares a list of companies offering attachment opportunities in Kenya to help students know where to apply when the need arises.

    TABLE OF CONTENTS

    This list is based on each firm's reputation in terms of its commitment to providing attachment and internships to Kenyan youth. Many who have interned in these organizations attest that they are some of the best companies to work with in Kenya. Additionally, most employers in the job market highly recommend hiring people who have interned in these companies.

    Nonetheless, the list is subjective and may not encompass all potential attachment options. Also, readers are encouraged to verify the current status of attachment programs with the respective companies, as offerings and conditions may change over time.

    Companies offering attachment opportunities in Kenya

    Numerous private and government-owned companies offer attachment opportunities in Kenya. These programs help students learn skills needed on the job market, explore their career interests, build a professional network, and understand workplace expectations. Some of the companies you can apply for attachment opportunities in Kenya are:

    • Safaricom PLC
    • TotalEnergies Kenya
    • United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF)
    • Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC)
    • Base Titanium
    • Central Bank of Kenya (CBK)
    • Kenya Electricity Generating Company PLC (KenGen)
    • KCB Bank Kenya Limited
    • Kenya Broadcasting Corporation (KBC)
    • Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA)
    • National Industrial Training Authority (NITA)
    • Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC)

    Most of these organizations have branches countrywide. Hence, you can be deployed to a branch within your location for your attachment or internship program. Read about the attachment opportunities they offer below. Some organizations publicly share qualification requirements and documents needed during the application process:

    1. Safaricom PLC

    • Address: Safaricom House, Waiyaki Way, Westlands, Nairobi
    • PO Box: 66827, 00800, Nairobi
    • Telephone: +254 722 00 4524/4260
    • General career queries email: hr@safaricom.co.ke

    The Safaricom internship program runs quarterly, i.e., from April to June, July to September, October to December, and January to March. The program accepts graduates with a gap year and 3rd or 4th-year university students.

    No applications are processed over email; they are only done through the Safaricom careers portal. Selected applicants undergo an assessment, and only those who pass the evaluation are shortlisted.

    Successful candidates receive an email confirming their acceptance into the program for the year. Also, the job placement opportunities after the internships depend on the availability of positions and the candidate's skills.

    3. UNICEF

    • Address: QR89+M93, United Nations Avenue, Nairobi
    • PO Box: 44145, Nairobi, Kenya

    • Email: nairobi@unicef.org

    The United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) offers attachment opportunities in Nairobi, Kenya, or outside the country. The program runs for six to 26 weeks. What's more, interns get monthly stipends. If necessary, UNICEF will provide the documents required for your visa.

    The UNICEF internship program has the following requirements:

    • You should be at least 18 years old.
    • You should be enrolled in an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD program or have graduated within the past two years.
    • You should be proficient in at least one of UNICEF's working languages: English, French, or Spanish.
    • You should have no immediate relatives (e.g., mother, father, sister, brother) working with UNICEF or any other relatives in your reporting line of authority.
    • You should have strong academic performance and motivation.

    How do you get an attachment?

    Most companies offering internships and attachment opportunities in Kenya require students to apply online on the company's career portal. They also require the applicant to submit an application letter, an introduction letter from your college or university, a Curriculum vitae (CV), a copy of your national ID, copies of transcripts and KCSE certificate, a Certificate of Good Conduct, your bank account details (a copy of your bank card or ATM), and an insurance cover (Group/Personal Accident).

    Where can you get IT attachment opportunities in Kenya?

    TotalEnergies Kenya, Safaricom PLC, and KCB Bank offer IT attachment and internship opportunities in Kenya.

    Does KCB offer attachment?

    KCB Bank offers college and university students six-month attachment opportunities annually. The online application on the KCB careers portal is free, and hard-copy applications are not allowed.

    How do you apply for an attachment online?

    Find out the internship or attachment application procedure of the company you will apply to. You can get this information by contacting the company or visiting its nearest branch.
