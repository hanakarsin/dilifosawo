    One of the skills that have accelerated technological advancement in the modern era is programming. Due to a massive demand for skilled program writers, most of the programming schools in Kenya have reworked their curriculums to match the global standards. Unlike a decade ago, you do not need to travel thousands of kilometres to be an excellent programmer.

    Coding teaches more than just writing programmes. It helps young learners to develop problem-solving skills, computational skills, critical thinking, and discipline. No matter the programming language you select, you are sure to gain these skills. All you need to do is to choose the right school.

    Top 10 programming schools in Kenya

    There are many schools to choose from if you are interested in learning programming languages. An ideal school should offer the course you would like to undertake while adhering to international standards. With that in mind, these are the best programming schools in Kenya.

    1. Moringa School

    Moringa School is one of the best programming schools in Nairobi. It offers some of the best courses with modules ranging from basic to complex â€” based on what the learner wants to learn. Therefore, the school is perfect for people who have never been to a programming class.

    Besides the diverse curriculum, the Nairobi-based school is market-driven. The students learn other aspects of programming, such as adapting to the ever-changing world. The students also learn more programming languages, making them well-equipped and perfect for the competitive job market.

    Moringa School has boot camps and events for programmers interested in sharpening their skills. These events are also ideal for people transitioning to programming from other careers.

    2. 2KO Kenya

    2KO Kenya offers diverse courses in programming and IT. The courses offered at this institute are globally recognised, and students from the school can work anywhere in the world. Also, 2KO Kenya has customised courses aimed at the Kenya market.

    The school has a similar approach to Revature Kenya, which is an e-learning platform. However, unlike Revature, 2KO Kenya has a physical address in Kenya for easier training. However, the main campus is in South Africa.

    The main courses taught are Linux, Microsoft, Cisco, CompTIA, Oracle, and Sun. Besides the curriculum, 2KO Kenya teaches students how to be entrepreneurs. Therefore, graduates can either choose to pursue careers in big tech companies or start their tech start-ups.

    3. Computer Pride

    Computer Pride is one of the oldest programming schools in Kenya. With more than three decades in operation, the school has trained thousands of programmers. The institute also has close to 20 main partners that give students and graduates a soft landing in the job market.

    The school teaches different coding courses such as Java SE, C#, and creating Android apps. Besides the classroom-based learning, Computer Pride has some of the best coding boot camps in Kenya. The best part about this college is that it has monthly intakes. The flexible schedule allows students to start learning, unlike waiting for the rigid school calendars.

    4. Techcamp Kenya

    Are you looking for a programming school that teaches through e-learning platforms and boot camps? If yes, Techcamp Kenya is a perfect school for you. The two modes (of learning) allow students to have flexible learning schedules, unlike following the regular school calendar.

    Besides the progressive learning modes, Techcamp Kenya does not have a rigid entry process. The school will not ask for grades like other institutes. The only threshold is to show dedication and more importantly, to exhibit love for technology.

    5. African Advanced Level Telecommunications Institute (AFRALTI)

    AFRALTI is a programming school with a keen interest in Python, unlike teaching all the coding languages. Apart from specialising in one language, the college has qualified instructors, which are also professionals working in different fields.

    AFRALTI is also unique in terms of the studentsâ€™ population. Unlike other colleges offering programming courses, it has other departments teaching other programs. As a student, you will interact with other students pursuing different skills and create connections outside the tech space.

    6. Kwetu Hub

    Kwetu Hub is a programming school based in Juja, Kiambu. The learning institute started as a business before branching to become a school. Although Kwetu Hub is now independent, it is skills-based. It is therefore perfect for students that want to learn the latest skills regarding programming.

    As a programming school, Kwetu Hub has instructors that are active in Kenyaâ€™s programming space. Therefore, they are knowledgeable about which skills are on-demand in Kenya and which are obsolete.

    At Kwetu Hub, you can learn iOS and Android app development, web app development and API development. Also, the college teaches how to run a business as a programmer.

    7. The eMobilis Mobile Technology Training Institute

    The eMobilis Mobile Technology Training Institute is a school teaching coding in Kenya but emphasises mobile apps. The most common courses are MIT, Android app development and USSD.

    Apart from being one of the biggest markets for programmers, the mobile programming niche is profitable, and students can easily create companies unlike seeking employment. Therefore, eMobilis also teaches students entrepreneurial courses.

    8. Africa Digital Media Institute (ADMI)

    ADMI is a school teaching digital media with a keen interest in solving African problems regarding technology. One of the major courses at this school is programming. Besides the teaching staff, the school is one of the best-equipped programming schools in Kenya.

    ADMI has two modes of studies. You can take any course and study through distance learning or regular school. Both of the two options are ideal for the programming students, thanks to the improved infrastructure.

    ADMI offers courses in app development. In addition to creating apps, you will also learn the business aspect of operating a digital company.

    9. The iLab Africa

    iLab Africa is an innovation hub affiliated to Strathmore University. Apart from being a research centre, it offers short courses on different niches, and one of the major study areas is programming. If you enrol in the institute, you will learn how to program in various languages.

    The school has programming events dedicated to students pursuing courses outside tech. Also, professionals can attend the events to learn more about cording and programming.

    10. Muva Academy

    Muva Academy is one of the coding schools in Nairobi that emphasis on practical learning. The instructors empower the students to be better coders and have marketable skills-set. They also mentor students on how to navigate the competitive job market.

    Muva Academy does not have a definite curriculum like other schools. The scope of the courses depends on what the learners want to know. However, the instructors can teach everything on app development both for iOS and Android ecosystems. The academy also has API Development courses. The lessons help the students to integrate payment options such as Lipa Na Mpesa to systems.
