    KCPE Results: Best 15 Candidates In 2020 National Exams

    - Education Cabinet Secretary Magoha acknowledged it was a difficult academic calendar for the candidates - The results showed public schools got 10 out of 15 top slots while female candidates took eight slots and the first three overall slots

    13 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Education Cabinet Secretary Magoha acknowledged it was a difficult academic calendar for the candidates

    - The results showed public schools got 10 out of 15 top slots while female candidates took eight slots and the first three overall slots

    - Girls performed better than boys in English and Kiswahili, while male candidates did better in mathematics, science and social studies

    The Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) examination results for the year 2020 are finally out.

    This was the first national examination in which 1,179,182 candidates wrote their papers after nearly one year break due to the coronavirus pandemic that saw schools closed in March 2020.

    "When COVID-19 pandemic befell our country, education sector was affected. We were at crossroads as this was new…many predicted doom for our sector….we decided to have phased reopening of schools," said Education Cabinet Secretary George Mogoha.

    Speaking during the release of the results at the Mtihani House on Thursday, April 15, Magoha acknowledged it was a difficult academic calendar for the candidates.

    "Many doomsayers predicted that 2020 candidates would perform dismally. The good news is that the performance has shown no remarkable difference from results in the previous years," he said.

    Here are the best 15 candidates, respective schools, and their scores;

    1. Mumo Faith - Karimwaindu Primary School - 433

    2. Wesonga Nanzala - Chogoria Girls 432

    3. Muriithi Angel – Maseno Girls Boarding 432

    4. Wanyonyi Samuel – Nzoia Sugar Company 431 marks

    5. Castro Williams - Crystal Hill Academy 431

    6. Tarus Chepkemboi - St Matthews Septonok 431

    7. Abiud Kipkurui- Lessos Hills Adventist 430

    8. Mwangi Margaret Waruguru – Karatina DEB -430

    9. Dennis Omondi – Nairobi Primary School 429

    10. Kiogora Joyce Nkatha – Chogoria primary 429

    11. Daniel Chris Mboya- Crystal Academy 428

    13. Jeff Mutugi – Friends Academy – 428

    14. Polycarp Kiyondi- Ober Boys 428
