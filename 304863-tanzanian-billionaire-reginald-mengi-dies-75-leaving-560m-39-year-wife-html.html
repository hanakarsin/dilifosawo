    Late tycoon Reginald Mengi's young widow stands to inherit part of his KSh 56 billion empire

    Amidst the sad and unexpected death of 75-year-old Tanzanian tycoon Reginald Mengi, netizens cannot help but suggest his 39-year-old widow stands to bag a huge chunk of his vast fortune. According to Forbes, the late billionaire's wealth is estimated at KSh 56 billion.

    08 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Amidst the sad and unexpected death of 75-year-old Tanzanian tycoon Reginald Mengi, netizens cannot help but suggest his 39-year-old widow stands to bag a huge chunk of his vast fortune.

    According to Forbes, the late billionaire's wealth is estimated at KSh 56 billion.

    Now, his Tanzanian fans are convinced former singer K-Lynn also known as Jacqueline Mengi might just become one of Africa's wealthiest women.

    Reginald owned a chain of businesses that ranged from media houses to bottling companies.

    The 75-year-old power house founded IPP Group which manufactured water and was contracted to provide bottles for the Coca Cola company.

    Also, he had businesses that dealt with real estate and mining.

    TUKO.co.ke understands the deceased's companies were a source of employment for around 5,000 people and was a major taxpayer.

    K-Lynn and Reginald have two sons together and the pair enjoyed the lavish trips and expensive homes while the tycoon was still alive.

    The former singer who also doubled as a model lived a life her peers could only dream of.

    Reginald who died on May 2, 2019, in Dubai was one who loved out loud and never shied away from professing his emotions.

    During the couple's last wedding anniversary in March, the billionaire wished he could have a chance to spend his life in heaven with none other than his lovely spouse.
