    Kenyan Newspaper Review for July 8: Business Tycoon Jimi Wanjigi Eying to Unseat Raila as ODM Leader

    The papers also reported on the demise of renowned journalist Hillary Ng'weno who bowed out aged 83 on Wednesday, July 7.

    1. The Standard

    The daily reported that agitated parents of killer Nakuru police officer on the run Caroline Kangogo pleaded with their daughter to turn herself in.

    After seeing their daughter in the headlines, Kangogo's father Barnaba Korir and her mother Leah Kangogo pleaded with her to surrender and not harm other more people.

    The duo wished their daughter, whom they said had been kind to them, would come out and confess her role in the murders in Nakuru and Kiambu.

    Kangogo's is accused of the murder of constable John Ogweno on Monday, July 5 and Peter Ndwiga on Tuesday, July 6.

    "My daughter, you are police who understands the law and under such circumstances, you know why it is important to surrender. Wherever you are please turn yourself in. We all want to know what happened and to avoid more bloodshed," said Korir.

    He said his daughter had been of good character and it remains a misery to know how she was caught up in such an ugly situation to warrant police to circulate her picture describing her as rogue, armed and dangerous.

    "She was brought up as a normal child and we are still shocked by the chilling, sudden turn of events. We received the news with great grief and want to extend our message of comfort to the families of the slain men," he added.

    Kangogo's mother said her daughter had been disciplined all her life and visited them frequently with goodies.

    2. Daily Nation

    The publication reported that billionaire Jimi Wanjigi wants to snatch the leadership of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) from Raila Odinga.

    Wanjigi's entry comes at a time when the party is also trying to broker a coalition with the ruling Jubilee Party ahead of the 2022 General Election.

    "I will be running for the ODM ticket and I will be taking on Raila and that will be my presidential ticket. As an ODM life member, I have what it takes to lead the party and the country into the future," said Wanjigi.

    Regarded as one of the election financiers and political fixer in the last two general elections, the announcement by the businessman to seek the ODM ticket might ruffle feathers within the party.

    3 The Star

    Opposition chief Raila Odinga has set the stage for a gruelling duel with Deputy President William Ruto and released snippets of his 2022 blueprint that will anchor his presidential run.

    Over the last few weeks, Raila is going flat out to reawaken his political bastions and conquer new territory as he angles for the top job with or without the Building Bridges Initiative.

    The former prime minister has been bolstered by reports President Uhuru Kenyatta has agreed to back his candidature — giving hopes to his team he could reap big from Mt Kenya — a region that has perennially voted against him.

    For the first time on Tuesday, Raila gave out bits and pieces of his political blueprint making national stability, the war against corruption, job creation, industrialisation and support for small business the centre of his presidential run.

    The High Court on Wednesday, July 7, granted Senate Speaker Kenneth Lusaka until July 28 to reach an out of court settlement with a woman seeking KSh 25 million for the upkeep of their unborn child.

    Justice Antony Nrima allowed Lusaka and the woman more time to negotiate the issues raised in the petition and record consent.

    This was after the speaker, through his lawyer Peter Wanyama admitted in court that he was the father of the unborn child.

    5. Taifa Leo

    DP Ruto is on the spot for granting a Dubai based company a tender to print United Democratic Alliance (UDA) branded wares instead of local manufacturers.

    This is perceived to be contrary to his hustler nation agenda that seeks to empower small and local businesses.

    However, the outfits secretary-general Veronica Maina insisted that the wares were bought and printed locally.
