    Engineering is a lucrative career with an increasing growth outlook in years to come. The job can be quite financially rewarding if you are truly skilled at what you do, get involved in cutting-edge projects in your field, and stay up to date with the latest advancements in the industry. With that said, find out the engineering courses Kenyan universities and colleges offer.

    TABLE OF CONTENTS

    Engineering courses are rigorous because, during your undergraduate study, you must learn facts and techniques and develop a meticulous way of thinking and applying the scientific method. Additionally, most schools encourage pursuing engineering internships or research experiences to practice your knowledge and skills and learn what it's like to be a real engineer.

    Top engineering courses in Kenya

    Engineering encompasses different disciplines, from theoretical understanding of processes (chemistry, mathematics, physics, technology, etc.) to design to research. If you are interested in becoming an engineer and ready to study, check out the types of engineering courses you can study in Kenya and choose one that best suits your dream career.

    1. Civil engineering courses

    Civil engineering involves designing, constructing, and maintaining the physical and naturally built environment, including roads, bridges, canals, dams, airports, sewage systems, pipelines, buildings, and railways.

    To enroll for undergraduate civil engineering courses, you must have a relevant diploma from an accredited institution and a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus), with at least a B (plain) mean grade for the total score in the four JAB cluster subjects and the following minimum grades in the individual cluster subjects: Mathematics C+, Physics C+, Chemistry C+, Geography or Biology, or group III or any group IV.

    A diploma in civil engineering requires a certificate with a credit pass in the relevant area of study with a minimum of two (2) years of working experience in the relevant field. You also need a KCSE mean grade of C (plain) and meet the cluster requirement Div III in O-Level and the cluster requirement of the program.

    To do a certificate in civil engineering, you need a KCSE mean grade of D+ (plus) and meet the cluster requirement Div. IV in O-Level and the cluster subject requirements of the program or have a pre-certificate with a pass in the relevant area of study.

    2. Architecture/structural engineering courses

    Architecture/structural engineering is a sub-discipline of civil engineering. The course teaches engineers how to design the 'bones and joints' that create the form and shape of human-made structures. Structural engineering is essential for ensuring buildings' and structures' safety, stability, and longevity.

    A degree in structural engineering requires a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus) and at least a C+ (plus) in each cluster subject: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology/any Group III/any Group IV/any Group V.

    3. Electrical engineering courses

    Electrical engineering courses equip students with the knowledge and technical skills to design and assess equipment, devices, and systems that use electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism.

    Suppose you wish to enroll in an electrical engineering certificate. In that case, you must have attained a minimum KCSE mean grade of D+ (plus) with at least a D plain in Mathematics and Physics/Chemistry. The minimum entry requirements for a diploma course is a KCSE mean grade of C- (minus) and a D+ (plus)in Mathematics and Physics/Physical Sciences.

    To be considered for an electrical engineering degree course, you should have attained a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus) and a minimum of C+ (plus)in the cluster subjects Physics, Chemistry, and English/Kiswahili.

    4. Mechanical engineering courses

    Mechanical engineering is a more general field that covers a wide range of automated systems and devices. Most universities offering mechatronics and mechanical engineering degrees in Kenya have automotive engineering as a branch or discipline of specialization.

    You need a minimum KCSE mean grade of B- (minus) to do a mechanical engineering degree and a minimum KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus) to pursue a mechanical engineering diploma in Kenya.

    5. Automotive engineering courses

    Automotive engineering focuses on helping a student understand different designs of automobiles and how the vehicles are developed and produced. At the end of the degree program, you should know how the distinct parts and systems go into making an automobile.

    To do a degree in automotive engineering in Kenyan universities and colleges, you need a minimum KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus) and at least C+ (plus) in the JAB cluster subjects, Math, English/Kiswahili, and Physics/Chemistry/Physical Sciences.

    A diploma in automotive engineering requires a KCSE mean grade of C- (minus), while a certificate requires a KCSE mean grade of D (plain).

    6. Telecommunication engineering courses

    Telecommunications engineering is a subfield of electronics engineering. The branch teaches how to design and devise communication systems at a distance. The work primarily ranges from basic circuit design to strategic mass developments.

    Engineers who are telecommunications specialists design voice, video, and data communications systems to keep users connected. To achieve this, they install equipment incorporating copper or fiber optic cabling, complex networks, and switching systems to transmit phone, cable, and broadband data.

    A certificate telecommunication engineering course requires a KCSE mean grade of D (plain), with a D (Plain) in Mathematics, Physics, and English. Meanwhile, a diploma requires a Certificate (Credit and above) in telecommunication engineering and a KCSE mean grade of C- (minus), with a C (plain) in English and a C- (minus) in mathematics and physics/physical sciences/electricity/drawing and design.

    Meanwhile, you need a Diploma (Credit and above) in telecommunication engineering and a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus), with a minimum of C+ (plus) in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, and English or Kiswahili to do a BSc. in telecommunication engineering in Kenya.

    7. Aeronautical engineering courses

    Aeronautical engineering involves research, design, development, construction, and aircraft testing. The field also covers investigation into aerodynamic characteristics of aircraft, including behaviors and related factors such as airfoil, control surfaces, lift, and drag.

    Aeronautical and astronautical engineering are the two major and overlapping branches of aerospace engineering. To pass aeronautical engineering courses, you must be highly competent in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. These subjects are rigorously tested throughout your program.

    To enroll in an aeronautical engineering degree in Kenya, you need a minimum KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus) and a C+ (plus) in Physics, English, and Mathematics.

    8. Software engineering courses

    Software engineering deals with designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software applications. Software engineers apply engineering principles and knowledge of programming languages to build software solutions for end users.

    Some of the specializations in software engineering are Mobile App Development, Web Programming/Web Design, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML), Data Analytics, Database Administrator (DBA), and Software Quality Testing (SQT) Automation.

    To study a software engineering degreein Kenya, most universities require a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus), with a C+ (plus) in Mathematics and Physics. The cluster subjects for this course include Math, Physics, Chemistry, and English language. A diploma in software engineering/computer science/IT is accepted.

    9. Agricultural and biosystems engineering courses

    Biosystems engineering applies engineering sciences to solve problems involving agriculture, environmental, and other biological production systems.

    Agricultural and biosystems engineers use their knowledge of mathematics, biological and physical sciences, and engineering principles to solve problems relating to producing, handling, and processing natural materials for food, feed, fiber, and fuel. These experts also design and develop machine systems and preserve the quality of natural resources and the environment.

    Career opportunities for graduates of this agricultural course are many and diverse. They design, develop, and test agricultural machine systems for processing food products. These experts can also create, develop, and manage crop irrigation systems.

    To enroll in an agricultural and biosystems engineering degree, you need a KCSE minimum mean grade of C+ (plus), with a C+ (plus) in Agriculture and Biology/Biological Sciences and a C (plain) in Chemistry/Physical Sciences, Mathematics/Geography, English/Kiswahili/ any Group II, III, IV or V subject.

    10. Textile engineering courses

    Textile engineering courses help you learn the development and manufacture of textile fabric used in the apparel industry. The course teaches applying scientific and engineering principles to the design and control all aspects of fiber, textile, and apparel processes, products, and machinery.

    Universities offering textile engineering courses in Kenya require a KCSE mean grade of C+ (plus), with a C+ (plus) in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and English/Kiswahili.
