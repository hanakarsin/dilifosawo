    Kibra by-election: Hassan Joho, Junet Mohammed pitch tent in Raila's backyard to campaign for Imran

    - ODM top leadership toured Kibra in vote hunting mission for its Imran Okoth - Joho and Junet joined Muslim faithful at Makina Mosque for Friday prayer - The team later visited Kibra fire victims where Imran distributed iron sheets

    15 Jun 2024, 00:00

    - ODM top leadership toured Kibra in vote hunting mission for its Imran Okoth

    - Joho and Junet joined Muslim faithful at Makina Mosque for Friday prayer

    - The team later visited Kibra fire victims where Imran distributed iron sheets

    Opposition party Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) on Friday, September 20, dispatched its top guns for a vote hunting mission in Kibra constituency ahead of upcoming by-election.

    The team led by deputy party leader and Mombasa governor Hassan Joho and Suna MP Junet Mohamed accompanied ODM candidate Bernard Okoth Imran to Makina fire victims where he distributed roofing sheets and household items.

    The politicians also had opportunity to join Muslim faithful for Friday prayers at Makina Mosque.

    Speaking at Junet urged supporters to turn out in large numbers to ensure Imran wins the seat so that he continues from where his late MP Ken Okoth, his brother left.

    The Orange party appointed Junet to lead its campaigns in Kibra by-election which will take place on November 7.

    "Imran will continue the good development work he was doing as Kibra CDF Manager. He has already shown he is a diligent public servant. Kibra needs him to continue steering the constituency," he said.

    The Kibra parliamentary seat fell vacant following the death of the area MP Ken Okoth on the afternoon of Friday, July 26, 2019.
