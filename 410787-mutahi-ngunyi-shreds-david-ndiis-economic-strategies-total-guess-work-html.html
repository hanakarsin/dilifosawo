    Controversial political analyst Mutahi Ngunyi has termed David Ndii's economic strategies as total guesswork.

    Taking to his official Twitter account, Ngunyi described Ndii as an intellectual prostitute who time and again has been advising the losing team.

    "It is not a tragedy that a Kikuyu psycho like David Ndii has now become an intellectual prostitute. The tragedy is this: he has always advised the team that loses," Ngunyi said.

    Deputy President William Ruto held a three-day consultative meeting over the weekend with a section of Mt Kenya politicians and renowned economists, including Ndii, where he presented his economic agenda for 2022.

    The second in command is seeking to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta when his second and last term comes to an end in 2022.

    He has embarked on an elaborate strategy to build a manifesto centred on ordinary people as he aggressively forges a formidable campaign machine.

    His behind-the-scenes team has expanded in the last month, bringing on board well-known economists, political experts and veteran political leaders from around the country.

    Apart from Ndii, the other professional think tanks include former Central Bank Governor Njuguna Ndung’u, former Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir, and Prof Larry Gumbe, a former ally of Raila.

    In a recent TV interview with KTN, Ndii said Ruto asked him and a few others to help him come up with an economic agenda that he will run on if he takes office in 2022.

    "The deputy president asked me and a couple of people a couple of weeks ago to help him structure some kind of grassroots economic agenda that he was to run on," the economist said.

    Ndii who is a Raila Odinga ally turned critic said that Ruto is more engaged with the needs of the bottom of the pyramid than the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader.
