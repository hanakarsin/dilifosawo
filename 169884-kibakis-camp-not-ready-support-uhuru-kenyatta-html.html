    Kibaki's PNU divided on supporting Uhuru's new party

    - President Uhuru Kenyatta is facing headache in his bid to form the Jubilee Alliance Party ahead of 2017 polls - Mwai Kibaki's PNU is opposed to the merger despite its chairman appearing at State House during Uhuru's announcement

    20 Aug 2024, 00:00

    - President Uhuru Kenyatta is facing headache in his bid to form the Jubilee Alliance Party ahead of 2017 polls

    - Mwai Kibaki's PNU is opposed to the merger despite its chairman appearing at State House during Uhuru's announcement

    READ ALSO: IEBC sets campaign spending limits for 2017 elections

    President Uhuru Kenyatta is facing headache from his predecessor’s, Mwai Kibaki, camp in his bid to form the Jubilee Alliance Party (JAP) ahead of the 2017 polls.

    Kibaki’s Party of National Unity (PNU) is opposed to the idea of folding up to merge with other parties under the Jubilee coalition umbrella to form the new party.

    Meru Governor Peter Munya has dismissed the claims that PNU has already agreed to dissolve and form JAP.

    During the announcement of the merger made by Uhuru at State House on Tuesday, August 9, PNU chairman John Kamama was among party representatives who were present to confirm the merger.

    However, Munya has dismissed the presence of the chairman saying the PNU top brass has not met and discussed the move, therefore rendering his presence a personal decision.

    READ ALSO: Musalia Mudavadi accuses Uhuru of killing democracy

    PNU is led by retired President Mwai Kibaki, being the party he used to retain his seat in the contested 2007 general election.

    A number of other parties under the Jubilee umbrella are also reluctant to fold up to form the JAP.

    The Kenya African National Union (KANU) has indicated it will not dissolve to form JAP. Its chairman, Gideon Moi, said that despite having an agreement with Jubilee since 2013, the independence party is not ready to merge with others.

    Ford People too is said to be opposed to the move despite one of its officials being at State House during the announcement.

    Already a section of leaders in Deputy President William Ruto’s camp have expressed their displeasure at the move by Ruto to dissolve his party, United Republican Party (URP).

    Among those who have indicated ditching Ruto is Bomet Governor Isaac Ruto. The governor has already formed his own Chama cha Mashinani (CCM) party.
